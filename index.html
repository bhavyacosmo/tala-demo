The Curriculum

About the Sessions

Facilitated in a convenient parallel to how you will teach, our sessions cover all the main concepts — delivered in a dynamic, playful, and sensory way.

🥁

Rhythm

Spoken rhythm, body percussion, rhythm with objects, and percussion — express music with your gross motor skills.

🎼

Music Ensemble

Use Orff instruments and learn how to engage everyone in a classroom to play music together in a percussion orchestra.

🎨

Composition

This creative session turns you and your children into music composers as you score music for movie or cartoon clips.

🎤

Voice

Soundscapes, vocal warmups, and voice modulations inspired by stories open your imagination to vocalize.

💃

Movement

Body movement inspired by music. Folk dances and play-based expression using spontaneous and choreographed movement.

🔗

Transdisciplinary Integration

Customized sessions integrating music & movement with language, sciences, math, and other subjects.

❓

Q&A

In-depth discussions, expert tips, creative ideas, and best practices to give you a fresh perspective and real solutions.

Got Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is this workshop for?

This workshop is best suited for Music teachers, homeroom/class teachers, performing arts teachers, subject teachers, special educators, and anyone working with children from ages 2 to 7.

If you teach older children and would like to use general music in the classroom, this workshop will give you several strategies that can easily be adapted for children older than seven, up to middle school years.

I am not a music teacher. How will this workshop help me?

The sessions introduce you to a play-based approach to teaching and learning. You will discover different aspects that may suit your work as a teacher:

  • Circle time: Transform your circle time into a beautifully creative and inclusive activity.
  • Integration: Cross-curricular connections with mathematics, reading, coding, social studies, and more.

How do I pay for the workshop?

What pedagogies will I learn at the workshop?

We will touch base on child-centric pedagogies such as the Orff Approach, Kodály Method, and other play-based concepts, ideas, and methods that work well with children. All approaches have been tried, tested, and actively used in our centres and schools.
